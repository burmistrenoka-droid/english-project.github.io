Traditional Japanese Houses

Architecture Features

Traditional Japanese houses are usually one or two-story wooden structures. The first thing you do when entering is remove your shoes. Characteristic elements like shōji sliding doors and tatami floor mats made from natural materials make even small spaces feel more spacious.

Average size: 60-90 m² • Height: 2.2-2.4m

Modern Adaptations

While traditional houses are less common in cities, their design principles influence modern Japanese architecture. The emphasis on natural light, ventilation, and connection to nature remains important in contemporary homes.

Japanese Cuisine

Popular Dishes

  • Sushi - vinegared rice with seafood
  • Ramen - noodle soup in flavorful broth
  • Tempura - battered and fried seafood/vegetables
  • Takoyaki - octopus balls

Spicy & Unique Foods

Japanese cuisine features spicy flavors like wasabi and spicy ramen. Unique dishes include fugu (pufferfish) and various fermented foods. Presentation is as important as taste.

Dining Etiquette

It's customary to say "itadakimasu" before eating and "gochisōsama deshita" after. Slurping noodles shows enjoyment. Never stick chopsticks upright in rice.

Japanese Culture & Clothing

Traditional Clothing

Kimono - formal traditional garment
Yukata - casual summer kimono
Haori - jacket worn over kimono
Katana - traditional Japanese sword

Imperial Family

Japan has the world's oldest continuous monarchy. The Emperor symbolizes the state while the Prime Minister handles government. The Imperial Palace in Tokyo is the primary residence.

Cherry Blossoms (Sakura)

Hanami Tradition

Every spring, Japanese people enjoy hanami (flower viewing) parties under cherry blossom trees. This tradition dates back over 1000 years and represents the beauty and fragility of life.

Best Viewing Times

Cherry blossoms typically bloom from late March to early May, starting in the south and moving north. The blooming period is short (about 2 weeks), making it a special event.
